# Naming Patterns and Strategies

## Creative Naming Categories

### Portmanteaus
Blend two words by merging their parts.
- **Examples**: Snapchat (snapshot + chat), Instagram (instant + telegram), Pinterest (pin + interest)
- **Strength**: Creates unique, memorable names with clear meaning
- **Tips**: Look for natural sound overlaps between words

### Puns and Wordplay
Use double meanings, homophones, or clever linguistic tricks.
- **Examples**: Fiverr (five + err/or), Lyft (lift), Tumblr (tumbler with vowel removed)
- **Strength**: Memorable and conversational
- **Tips**: Consider pronunciation and how the pun relates to the product

### Creative Alliteration
Repeat initial consonant sounds for memorability.
- **Examples**: PayPal, Coca-Cola, Best Buy, TikTok
- **Strength**: Easy to remember and pronounce
- **Tips**: Choose sounds that feel natural together

### Metaphorical Names
Use evocative words that represent what the product does or feels like.
- **Examples**: Stripe (payment rails), Ripple (spreading effect), Nest (home comfort)
- **Strength**: Creates emotional resonance and brand personality
- **Tips**: Choose metaphors that align with product values

### Abstract Invented Words
Create entirely new words that sound appealing.
- **Examples**: Spotify, Etsy, Hulu, Zillow
- **Strength**: Highly unique, easier to trademark
- **Tips**: Ensure pronounceability and avoid negative associations in other languages

### Modified Real Words
Take existing words and modify spelling or structure.
- **Examples**: Flickr, Scribd, Tumblr, Grindr
- **Strength**: Familiar yet unique
- **Tips**: Remove vowels strategically or change endings

### Compound Words
Combine two complete words.
- **Examples**: Facebook, YouTube, Mailchimp, Dropbox
- **Strength**: Clear meaning, easy to understand
- **Tips**: Ensure the combination feels natural

### Number Integration
Incorporate relevant numbers meaningfully.
- **Examples**: 23andMe (chromosomes), 99designs, Auth0
- **Strength**: Can convey specific meaning or create uniqueness
- **Tips**: Only use if numbers have genuine relevance to the product

## Domain Naming Strategies

### Base Domain Patterns
1. **Direct**: [name].com (ideal but rare)
2. **Prefix patterns**:
   - get[name].com (action-oriented)
   - try[name].com (trial/testing focused)
   - use[name].com (utility focused)
   - my[name].com (personal/ownership)
   - go[name].com (action/movement)
3. **Alternative TLDs**: [name].ai, [name].io, [name].co

### Domain Hacks
Use TLD as part of the name (e.g., del.icio.us, bit.ly)
- **Caution**: Can be harder to remember and type

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Hard to spell from hearing**: Avoid ambiguous phonetics
- **Multiple valid spellings**: Could split your traffic
- **Negative meanings in other languages**: Always check
- **Too generic**: Harder to trademark and brand
- **Too similar to competitors**: Creates confusion
- **Difficult pronunciation**: Limits word-of-mouth growth
