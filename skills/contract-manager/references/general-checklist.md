# General Contract Checklist

> **Tier System**
>
> - **CRITICAL**: Must be present. Missing critical items BLOCK document generation.
> - **IMPORTANT**: Should be present. Missing items generate WARNINGS in the review.
> - **OPTIONAL**: Nice to have. Missing items generate SUGGESTIONS.

---

## CRITICAL

- [ ] **Parties Identification** — Full legal names, entity types (LLC, Corp, individual), registered addresses, authority to contract
- [ ] **Scope and Obligations** — Clear description of each party's obligations, deliverables, performance standards
- [ ] **Consideration and Payment** — What each party gives and receives, payment amounts, schedule, method, currency
- [ ] **Term** — Effective date, duration, renewal provisions (auto-renewal, opt-out)
- [ ] **Termination** — Termination for cause (with specific triggers), termination for convenience, notice periods, cure periods, effect of termination on surviving provisions
- [ ] **Representations and Warranties** — Each party's representations about authority, compliance, accuracy of information
- [ ] **Governing Law** — Which jurisdiction's law governs interpretation, venue for disputes

## IMPORTANT

- [ ] **Indemnification** — Mutual or one-way, scope, procedures (notice, control of defense, cooperation), caps
- [ ] **Limitation of Liability** — Cap on total liability, exclusion of indirect/consequential damages, carve-outs (IP infringement, confidentiality breach, willful misconduct)
- [ ] **Confidentiality** — Definition, obligations, exclusions, term, survival
- [ ] **Dispute Resolution** — Escalation procedure, mediation, arbitration vs. litigation, venue, fee allocation
- [ ] **Force Majeure** — Definition of qualifying events, notification requirements, mitigation obligations, right to terminate if extended
- [ ] **Assignment** — Restrictions on assignment, change of control provisions, consent requirements
- [ ] **Notice Provisions** — Required delivery method, addresses, deemed receipt timing
- [ ] **Insurance** — Types and minimum coverage amounts required

## OPTIONAL

- [ ] **Entire Agreement** — Supersedes prior agreements, no oral modifications
- [ ] **Severability** — Invalid provisions don't void the whole contract
- [ ] **Waiver** — Failure to enforce doesn't waive future enforcement rights
- [ ] **Counterparts** — Agreement may be signed in counterparts, electronic signatures valid
- [ ] **Third-Party Beneficiaries** — Statement that no third-party beneficiaries are intended (or identification of any)
- [ ] **Survival** — Which provisions survive termination/expiration
- [ ] **Publicity** — Whether parties can reference the relationship publicly, approval requirements
- [ ] **Compliance with Laws** — General obligation to comply with applicable laws and regulations
