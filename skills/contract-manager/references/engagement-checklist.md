# Engagement/Consulting Agreement Checklist

> **Tier System**
>
> - **CRITICAL**: Must be present. Missing critical items BLOCK document generation.
> - **IMPORTANT**: Should be present. Missing items generate WARNINGS in the review.
> - **OPTIONAL**: Nice to have. Missing items generate SUGGESTIONS.

---

## CRITICAL

- [ ] **Scope of Work** — Detailed description of services, deliverables, milestones, and acceptance criteria. Should reference an Exhibit or Schedule for specifics.
- [ ] **Deliverables Definition** — "Deliverables" must be defined if used anywhere in the contract (IP, indemnification, etc.). Undefined terms create enforceability gaps.
- [ ] **Compensation and Payment Terms** — Rates/fees, payment schedule, invoicing procedures, late payment consequences, expense reimbursement
- [ ] **IP and Work-for-Hire Ownership** — Clear assignment of IP created during engagement, work-for-hire designation where applicable, license grants
- [ ] **Independent Contractor Status** — Explicit statement that contractor is not an employee, no benefits entitlement, tax responsibility
- [ ] **Term and Termination** — Start date, end date or conditions, termination for cause, termination for convenience with notice period, effect of termination on pending work
- [ ] **Confidentiality** — Obligations regarding client's confidential information, survival period
- [ ] **Pre-existing IP Carve-out** — Contractor's pre-existing IP remains theirs, listed in schedule or described categorically, license granted to client for use in deliverables

## IMPORTANT

- [ ] **Time Commitment / Hours** — For retainer engagements: approximate monthly hours and overage rate. Protects consultant from unlimited demands and client from under-delivery.
- [ ] **Representations and Warranties** — Contractor has authority, work will be original, no conflicts, professional standard of care. Use "gross negligence" not "negligence" in indemnification triggers.
- [ ] **Indemnification** — Each party's indemnification obligations, procedures, caps. Trigger should be "gross negligence or willful misconduct" not simple "negligence."
- [ ] **Limitation of Liability** — Cap on damages (typically 6 months' fees). Exclusion of consequential damages. Carve-outs should be limited to avoid swallowing the cap.
- [ ] **Non-solicitation** — Restrictions on soliciting each other's employees/clients. For investment firms, narrow to "direct, substantive contact" to avoid overbroad restrictions.
- [ ] **Insurance Requirements** — Specify minimums (e.g., $1M general liability) rather than "reasonably appropriate." If company requires additional coverage, company should reimburse.
- [ ] **Transition Assistance Carve-out** — Transition assistance should be excluded if consultant terminates for cause or non-payment.
- [ ] **Change Order Process** — How scope changes are requested, approved, and priced
- [ ] **Dispute Resolution** — Mediation, arbitration, or litigation; venue; governing law
- [ ] **Subcontracting** — Whether contractor can use subcontractors, approval requirements

## OPTIONAL

- [ ] **Residual Knowledge** — Consultant retains general skills, techniques, and know-how (including written notes). Distinct from confidential information and company IP.
- [ ] **AI Tools Provision** — If consultant uses AI tools with client data: approved tools list, training opt-out requirements, breach notification, liability shield for third-party tool failures.
- [ ] **Exhibit A (Scope of Work)** — Separating scope into an exhibit allows updates without amending the main agreement.
- [ ] **Non-compete** — Restrictions on competitive work during/after engagement
- [ ] **Background Check/Clearance** — Security clearance or background check requirements
- [ ] **Equipment and Tools** — Who provides equipment, software licenses, workspace
- [ ] **Status Reporting** — Frequency and format of progress reports
- [ ] **Acceptance Testing** — Process and timeline for client to accept/reject deliverables
- [ ] **Force Majeure** — For month-to-month contracts with short termination notice, may be unnecessary since either party can just terminate.
- [ ] **Key Personnel** — Named individuals required for the engagement
- [ ] **Attorney-in-Fact** — If included, change "unable or unwilling" to just "unable" (incapacity, death, unreachable). Prevents company from overriding active disagreement.
