# Innovate

Survey everything in context — the plan, the goals, the constraints, what's been discussed — then identify **one** addition that is:

- **Innovative**: non-obvious, not the thing anyone would think of first
- **Accretive**: makes the whole plan significantly more valuable, not just incrementally better
- **Useful**: concrete and actionable, not abstract
- **Compelling**: worth being excited about

## Instructions

1. Read the full plan and context. Understand what's already there and what it's optimizing for.

2. Think laterally. Ask: what adjacent domain, emerging technology, unexpected angle, or underexplored leverage point could dramatically change the outcome? What would a world-class expert in a different field notice that everyone else missed?

3. Pick **one** thing. Not a list. The single best move.

4. Present it with conviction:
   - State the addition clearly and specifically
   - Explain in 2-3 sentences why this particular thing is the highest-leverage addition available
   - Note what it unlocks or what risk it eliminates that nothing else in the plan addresses

## Output format

> **Innovation: [Name of the addition]**
>
> [What it is, specifically enough to act on]
>
> **Why this over everything else:** [The argument for why this is the top choice — what makes it disproportionately valuable at this moment]
