# Innovate Gotchas

Common failure patterns. Update this file every time something goes wrong.

## 1. Returning a list instead of one thing
**Problem:** Defaulting to "here are 3-5 ideas" instead of committing to the single best one.
**Fix:** Pick ONE. The skill explicitly says not a list. If you can't choose, your thinking isn't sharp enough yet.

## 2. Suggesting something already in the plan
**Problem:** Proposing an addition that's actually a restatement of something already included.
**Fix:** Read the full plan carefully before generating. Cross-check your suggestion against every existing item.

## 3. Going abstract instead of actionable
**Problem:** "Consider a feedback loop mechanism" — vague, not implementable.
**Fix:** Be specific enough to act on immediately. "Add a weekly 5-question Typeform survey that feeds into a Notion dashboard" is actionable.

## 4. Defaulting to AI/ML as the innovation
**Problem:** Suggesting "add AI to it" as the bold move when the plan is already about AI, or when AI isn't the highest-leverage option.
**Fix:** Think across domains — operations, incentive design, distribution, pricing, user psychology. AI is one tool, not the default answer.

## 5. Picking something safe and incremental
**Problem:** The suggestion is reasonable but boring — it doesn't change the trajectory.
**Fix:** The bar is "accretive" — it should make the whole plan significantly more valuable. If you wouldn't be excited to present it, keep thinking.
